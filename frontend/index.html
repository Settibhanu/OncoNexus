How OncoTrace works
Six stages from raw genomic data to diagnostic insight
①
Data acquisition
Gene expression profiles from TCGA-BRCA (cancer) and GTEx (healthy breast tissue) feed the system.
②
Network construction
Genes and proteins become nodes. STRING database interactions become weighted edges forming a biological graph.
③
Self-supervised GNN
A Graph Neural Network trains on healthy samples only — learning the baseline structure of normal biological networks.
④
Perturbation detection
Cancer samples are compared to the learned baseline. Genes with high deviation scores are flagged as perturbed.
⑤
Biomarker discovery
Top perturbed genes are ranked as candidate biomarkers — including both known genes and novel discoveries.
⑥
Clinical insight
Risk scores and network visualisations provide actionable diagnostic signals for earlier, more precise detection.
